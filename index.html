Pet`s Paw

Landing page for pet`s adoption project.

About the project

The Pet's Paw project is a charity project that primarily aims to help Ukrainian shelters find new owners for homeless pets.

The Project in numbers:

  • 5 years
  • 20 shelters in collaboration
  • 10+ partners
  • 1200+ pets adopted

Design with

  • Photoshop
  • Figma

My role:

  • to convince people that taking the pets from shelters is absolutely safe and normal
  • to build an engaging and user-friendly infrastructure for the target audience
  • to create a safe and cosy mood of the landing page, which transmits main values of the project

Target audience

The audience is quite wide, so the task was to create a design that would be understandable to everyone.

  • Caring people

    People aged 18 to 80 who understand the problem of shelters, are responsible and have the financial ability to keep animals.

  • Parents

    Parents from 25 to 40 whose children ask for a pet.

  • Young people

    People from 18 to 30 who are just getting interested in the topic of shelters and can adopt a pet.

01

Secondary research

I started secondary research by identifying a problem.

  • Problem:

    Stereotypes that every animal in a shelter is a creature with a broken psyche, thousands of diseases, and an inability to share a roof with a human.

  • Task:

    To build a structure, that will educate users and normilize the idea of pet adoption among the target audience, while destroying the myths about homeless pets.

  • Solution:

    • I designed a landing page that educates people on the issues surrounding shelter animals, dispelled misconceptions about "street" animals, and helps people to find a new furry friend.

02

Brainstorm

Disney's Creative Strategy: The Dreamer, The Realist and The Critic.

The given strategy was instrumental in helping me generate a range of ideas for my project and identify the most effective strategy. Thanks to this method, I was able to approach the project with a well-informed and robust strategy, ultimately leading to creation of Pet’s Paw landing page you might see below.

03

Wireframing

This is the phase where all the ideas get wireframed and added to the backlog for further structuring.

  • Lo-fi wireframing

    Lo-fi wireframes can reflect a product’s basic architecture and features, showing where the content and visuals blocks will be placed.

  • Hi-fi wireframing

    Hi-fi is well-researched and presents a more precise look and functionality of the future product.

04

Moodboard

After the wireframes were approved, I moved on to the moodboard, where I showed the idea and style of the project.

For the stylistic design, I proposed to use a minimalist style with hand-drawn elements and accents on pastel shades, which would be modern and add playfulness and lightness to our landing page at the same time.

Typography

I chose the Paytone One with wide, round bowls for the header on the home screen and Monserrat sans serif for the body text.

I’m looking for a new home!

Our main task is to find homes for animals in shelters.

  • H1 - Paytone One 96px
  • H2 - Montserrat 46px
  • H3 - Montserrat SemiBold 24px
  • Main text - Montserrat Bold 16 px
  • Main text - Montserrat Regular 16 px

Colors

For the color palette, I suggested using pastel shades.

  • The main color:

    Purple - for buttons, main details and elements. Purple has the ability to calm, eliminates the negative effects of stress.

  • The accent color:

    Pink is a nurturing, playful, and nostalgic color that takes people back to their childhoods.

  • Additional color:

    • Cream (for the background) - cannot cause negative emotions in a person and allows to focus on the content of the page.
